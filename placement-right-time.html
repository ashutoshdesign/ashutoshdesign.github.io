Ensuring on-time deliveries

Indian Railways has recently come under intense criticism for large run time delays of trains, which often disrupts passengers' meal timings. How might we then improve the online catering experience of travellers?

This project was done as a part of the UXplorer 2017, a national-level student design challenge, during July-August 2017.

Project Overview

Delays in Indian railways is a regular affair, with trains getting late to upto 12 hours. In such a scenario, delivering food to passengers on-time is a challenge.

"Right Time" is an attempt at solving this problem. It is a time-based food delivery app for train travellers with an innovative and hasslefree user experience.

Contextual Interviews

Now that I had identified information gaps from secondary research, I set out onto the field to interview travellers to dive deeper into the problem. I conducted semi-structured interviews with 8 people, with the following questions to guide me through it-

  1. How often do you travel in trains? (No. of co-passengers, Class of travel)
  2. Describe your last lunch/dinner experience of your last train journey.
  3. What are the challenges that you face related to food during train journey?

Empathy Mapping

I quickly formulated all the data from 8 interviews onto empathy maps to study people's responses and identify their goals, emotions and motivations.

Introducing "Right Time"

Deciding What To Eat Just Got Easier

Browse quickly through a wide range of choiciest cuisines in a centralised menu with a convenient "Veg" filter to sort vegetarian cuisines.

Stay aware of your purchase

On adding an item to the cart, no. of items and total amount pops-up below. As you scroll down to explore more, ham menu appears again indicating the no. of items in the cart.

seamless navigation

The app has a Float Action Button for ham menu facilitating quick "one-hand navigation" through the app. Only the most important tasks for the user are included in the ham menu.

detecting pnr automatically

No need to fumble through your inbox searching for the IRCTC SMS, when Right Time automatically detects the PNR no. of your upcoming journey from the SMS. Though one can also enter it manually.

How it works?

Add food items to cart

Browse through a diverse range of food items quickly and add required quanity to cart

Review your order

Make last minute changes to your order and review the billed amount

Enter contact details

Contact details and PNR appear by default in the fields. Not you? Simply click and edit.

Select delivery slot

Choose delivery time of your convenience. Journey dates appear according to previously detected PNR.

Customise your order

Want your food less spicy? No worries. Customise the order according to your needs.

Keep track of your food

Track your order status after payment and confirmation. Get driver details once the order is out for delivery.

What if internet connection goes weak

The app also works during weak internet connection. It works in offline mode where you book your meal via SMS.

Learnings Recap

Through this project, I learnt how to convert insights from the research into meaningful products.

I also learnt various ideation techniques, how to make context-scenarios, design navigation, wireframes and prototypes. I learnt various tools of the trade like SketchApp and Marvel.

This project won "Silver medal" in UXplorer 2017, a national-level student design challenge.
