Hello!
I'm Ashutosh Agrawal,
a prefinal year undergraduate design student at Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati.

Born in a small town in India, I'm a Delhi Public School, Vindhyanagar pass-out, who managed to get into one of the premier institutions for design and engineering in my country.

My interest in design began from an early age when I used to dream up bizarre inventions or figure out what made a machine tick so that I could reimagine it to make it better. When I learnt that there was indeed a whole field of study that aligned with my interests and approached it in a scientific way, I knew I had to try it. My experiments in design were initially limited to creating graphics and user interfaces through my own instincts.

However, when I spent a couple of months interning at 1mg.com, India's largest health platform recognised by the United Nations and one of the top 10 start-ups of 2017 in India, it gave me whole new perspective as to what design really consisted of. This experience sparked my interest in design research. Now, I search hungrily for new technologies and skills that could help me face newer and more involving challenges. In the end, I hope that my work would influence the way designers think and be a part of products that people all over the world use.

Design, however, does not occupy all my time. I'm a sports enthusiast with a love for Squash and a nature lover who enjoys long road trips to exotic locations. My experience as Head Boy in school inspired me to take up leadership roles in college as well. I'm currently the Institute Secretary of Squash Club and have represented my institute at Inter-IIT Sports Meet. I also hold the post of Branch Representative of the Department of Design. These qualitites of sportsmanship, adventurism and leadership often tend to seep into my professional life where I never hesitate to shoulder reponsibilities and am not afraid to take risks.
