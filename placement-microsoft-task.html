Empowering students to achieve more

Digital transformation will bring about an inexorable change in educational experiences. How might we then design tools for students to support learning in future classrooms?

This project was done in 2018 as a part of “Mircrosoft Placement Task" in a duration of 3 days.

Project Overview

The educational practices of the traditional classroom are no longer effective. The modern-day classroom is more centered on students and teachers will take the role of facilitators and guides instead of being mere providers of knowledge.

'Slate App' is a digital learning tool for school students which supports meaningful, personalised, collaborative and organised learning. The app has been designed for a scenario where tablets like Microsoft Surfacebook would be ubiquitous in smart classrooms.

Discovery

“Understanding problem statement”

The problem statement encouraged me to think more about how classrooms in the recent future would lool like. So, I scoured through the literature to take a peek into the future of learning. Some recurring ideas from literature research talked about Interactive Smartboards/Projectors, Augmented Reality, Personalized Learning, E-learning platforms, Data Analytics and Cloud-based learning.

A view of the future classroom with tablets and smartboards

“Understanding the current learning experience”

I used mind-maps to list down various aspects of classroom learning and understand the relationship between them. The insights from the on-going literature research made the maps more dense and connected.

Using mind-maps to understand how learning takes place in schools

Analysis

“Identifying problems using context-scenarios”

Using the insights from my initial discovery phase, I sketched a context-scenario to visualize break-down points in the learning process in a future classroom. The learning tool I was about to design would aim to bridge those gaps.

Storyboarding scenarios identify break-points in future classroom learning.

Analysis

“Reframing problem statement”

Based on my learnings so far, I reframed the problem statement into a pithy question-

"How might we design a digital tool which supports meaningful, personalised, collaborative and organised learning for modern-day school students?"

“A tech-savvy high school kid”

To concretise my context and the problems associated with it, I sketched a user persona. I have chosen to design for a digitally-savvy high school student attending a modern edu-tech equipped school.

Meet the character in my story
