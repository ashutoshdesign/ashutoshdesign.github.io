Empowering youth with Sex Education

As many as 39 crimes against women are reported every hour in India. Can we trace its roots in the social norms of Indian society? Is education a powerful enough weapon to bring about a change in these norms?

This project was done as a part of “Systems Approach to Design" course under Prof. Pratul Kalita during January-April 2018.

Project Overview

In a conservative setting like India where sex is still a taboo, it is a challenge to empower the youth with accurate knowledge and skills to protect themselves and make informed decisions pertaining to their sexual and reproductive health (SRH).

In this project, we design a “system of smartphone apps" that improves access to quality SRH information for urban adolescents with an engaging and innovative user experience. I collaborated with Abhishek Kanthed during the entire process.

The first step to any design methodology starts with understanding the domain of the problem.

Literature Research

During the initial stage of the project, we studied numerous articles, reports and research papers to study related systems and identify problems in the existing system. We were surprised to know about the numerous dimensions and benefits of sex-education.

User Research

After a basic understanding of this domain, we conducted a survey with 18 students aged between 14-18 years to understand their perception and the current media of delivering SRH education.

Wrong perception of students on sex-education.

Unreliable sources of knowledge especially social media and internet searches.

Information exchange with peers instead of parents or other adults.

We conducted semi-structured interviews with 2 biology teachers, 2 parents and 2 doctors to take their opinion into account."The most shocking discovery for us was that even doctors and teachers perceived that sex-education promotes promiscuity".

Healthy Me, Healthy Us

"A comic-strip based app for 13-16 year olds"

Comic-strips as a learning space

Young teens have shown increased interest towards comics. They contain engaging stories, characters and visual imagery. “Comic-strips” would be a perfect medium to deliver knowledge in this context. "Healthy Me, Healthy us" is a concept app using "comic-strips" as a learning space.

Enable entertaining blend of of facts and stories.

Implicit means of knowledge delivery with relatable stories and characters.

Help develop a positive body image with visual imagery

"A netflix of comics"

This vertical houses comic-strips covering an array of topics on puberty, body development and communication. According to the experience level of the reader, only a certain number of comics are released at a time. The reader needs to earn experience points or "stars" to unlock the next level. User earns stars by completing more comics and performing well in evaluations (Even the evaluations are interesting! More on it later). This "gamifies" the user experience.

Concept Homescreens- 'Gamifying user experience'

"Interactive Search"

Users find information on their daily queries which they struggle hard to find on internet. The search provides a concise answer to the question and also suggests relevant comics to the user.

"Need for appreciation- a comic-strip mockup"

We designed a sample comic-strip aimed at clearing misconceptions and building self-confidence. "The protagnist in this story is influenced by stereotypical TV commercials. While discussing her motivations and desires with a friend, she comes to realize better ways of earning appreciation."

Sample comic-strip (Credits- Ankita Patra)

"Evaluate thyself"

We introduced a self-evaluation feature at the end of each comic-strip to consolidate the values gained from the story. The reader is posed with a question at the end of each story. Based on the option chosen by the reader, the story takes the corresponding turn- positive or negative. This facilitates self-introspection by the reader.

Facilitating 'Self-introspection'

Be Real, Be Ready

"A trialogue chat based mobile app for 17-18 year olds"

Trialogue as a learning space

A trialogue voice user interface has shown to enhance learning. We apply this concept in the context of sex-education. The working of this chatbot has been explained in the figure below. A trialogue consists of two virtual agents and a user. While one virtual agent delivers knowledge to the user, the other virtual agent matches user's mental model each time, thus empathizing with the user. This keeps user from loosing interest. The information flows as a conversation or "gossip". This increases user engagement.

Exploiting their love for gossip by delivering information via conversations.

Conversations teach knowledge implementation in real-life scenarios

Learning with minimum academic load and maximum engagement

Interact via Search

The student may start interacting with the chatbot by posing a question. The “supporting” virtual agent understands the context of question and confirms with the user. The "informative" virtual agent then delivers knowledge in the form of conversation or gossip.

Interact via Explore

If the student doesn’t have a query, the virtual agents can themselves start some gossip. In the process, they take the views of the student into account by asking questions. This way different kinds of scenarios can be delivered to the students in the form of a scenario.
